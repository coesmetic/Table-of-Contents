Low Carb Food List

1. Meats and Seafood

  • Beef, Pork, and Lamb: High in protein and virtually carb-free.
  • Poultry: Chicken, turkey, and duck, especially when unbreaded.
  • Fish: Salmon, trout, tuna, sardines, and mackerel.
  • Shellfish: Shrimp, crab, and lobster, typically low in carbs but check specific types.

2. Eggs

  • Eggs are nutrient-dense, low in carbs (less than 1g per egg), and provide healthy fats and proteins.

3. Vegetables (Non-Starchy)

  • Leafy Greens: Spinach, kale, and lettuce.
  • Cruciferous Vegetables: Broccoli, cauliflower, Brussels sprouts.
  • Other Low-Carb Vegetables: Zucchini, asparagus, bell peppers, cucumber, and mushrooms.

4. Dairy Products

  • Cheese: Most cheeses are low in carbs, like cheddar, Swiss, and feta.
  • Greek Yogurt: Opt for plain, unsweetened varieties in moderation.
  • Heavy Cream and Butter: Minimal carbs but high in fat.

5. Nuts and Seeds

  • Low-Carb Nuts: Almonds, walnuts, macadamia nuts, and pecans.
  • Seeds: Flaxseeds, chia seeds, and pumpkin seeds, which are also high in fiber.

6. Oils and Fats

  • Oils: Olive oil, avocado oil, and coconut oil, all with zero carbs.
  • Avocado: A whole food source of healthy fat and fiber with minimal carbs.

7. Berries (in Moderation)

  • Low-GI Berries: Strawberries, raspberries, and blackberries are lower in carbs compared to other fruits.

8. Beverages

  • Water: The best zero-carb option.
  • Unsweetened Tea and Coffee: Black coffee and green tea are low-carb, but avoid added sugars or milk.

9. Sweeteners (Carb-Free)

  • Stevia and Monk Fruit: Zero-carb, natural sweeteners often used in low-carb diets.

For each item, portion size and preparation can impact net carbs, so checking specific labels or using a carb-tracking tool can be helpful for accuracy.
