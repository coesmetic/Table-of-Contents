Introduction

Carbohydrates are a primary source of energy in the human diet, essential for supporting bodily functions and physical activity. Structurally, they are organic compounds made up of carbon, hydrogen, and oxygen, and are classified into three main types: sugars, starches, and fibers. Sugars, also known as simple carbohydrates, provide quick energy, while starches and fibers, or complex carbohydrates, deliver sustained energy and play crucial roles in digestion and heart health. Commonly found in foods like grains, fruits, and vegetables, carbohydrates are broken down into glucose, which cells use for fuel. While they are vital for energy, the type and amount of carbohydrate consumed are important for overall health.
